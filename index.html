Car fuel meter

Motivating scenario The fuel gauge of even the most advanced cars on the world’s streets gives only a rough reading of the amount of fuel in the tank. The thickness of the needle, the non-linearity of the gauge, all make for a very inaccurate estimate of the amount of fuel remaining. This might jeopardize the vehicle's running when on a highway with petrol pumps few and far between. Summary A meter that shows the exact amount of fuel remaining Problem statement Design a fuel metering system that can report the exact amount of fuel in the tank. The amount should be reported in a digital readout and should be in units of either liters or in kms at average/current consumption.

Smart water heater

Motivating scenario
Traditional immersion rod heaters are a combination of bad design and a total absence of all conveniences and smarts. They are difficult to clip on to the bucket, they touch the sides of the (plastic) bucket when hot, they can be started dry, they would supply heat while the water boils away....the list of absent conveniences is endless, resulting in frustration at best and accidents at worst.
Problem statement
Can one have a bucket water heater that would address the above issues?
Summary
Water heater that prevents dry heating, allows temp control, alerts user, etc. Engineering design involved.
